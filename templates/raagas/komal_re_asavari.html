Raag Komal Rishabh Asavari

This Raag is a morning melody. It belongs to the Bhairavi thaat/mode.It is also known as Asavari Todi. Raag Komal Rishabh Asavari uses all seven notes. It omits ga/ and ni/ in the aroha(ascent) but uses all seven notes in avaroha(descent).

Swaras Gandhar and Nishad Varjya in Aaroh. Pancham Varjya in Avroh. Rishabh, Gandhar, Dhaivat and Nishad Komal. Rest All Shuddha Swaras.
Jati Audhav-Shadhav Vakra
Thaat Bhairavi
Vadi/Samvadi Dhaivat/Rishabh
Time (9AM to 12 Noon): 2nd Prahar of the day: Din ka Dwitiya Prahar
Vishranti Sthan S; m; P; d; - d; m; r;
Mukhya-Ang m P d S' ; r' n d m g r ; g r S;  
Aaroh-Avroh S r m P d S' - S' r' n d m P d m g r g r ,n ,d r S;

Raag Yaman is s one of the first ragas a Hindustani classical student learns and is considered to be one of the most fundamental ragas in the tradition.Listed below are some basic lessons which every student must checkout ...

Raag or Raaga:
The melody with an overall mood or environment producing a lasting impression while entertaining or recreating the audience. A combination of different musical notes or Swaras in a particular sequence of ascending and descending makes a Raag.

Rasas or Bhavas:
There are 9 Rasas or Bhavas described in the Hindustani Classical Music/Dance forms: Bhakti (Devotional), Karuna, Veer, Veebhatsa, Vichitra, Hasya (Humour), Virah, Vairagya and Krodh (Anger).

Sargam:
The composition which uses the names of the notes instead of words.

Aaroh-Avroh:
Aaroh of a Raag is the sequence of the notes in ascending order between Madhya Shadja (S) and Tar Shadja (S'). Avroh of a Raag is the sequence of notes in descending order between Tar Shadja (S') and Madhya Shadja (S).

Alankar:
Alankars literally meaning jewels, are combination of the notes of a Raag. Alankars follow a particular pattern of combination of notes like an ornament and are very effective while rendering Taans.

Alap:
It is the development of a Raag through improvisation while observing all the laws of the Raag.

Antara:
It is the second stanza of a composition, usually including and emphasizing the upper notes of the Raag.

Chota Khayal:
A composition in medium to fast tempo that usually follows the bada khayal.

Bada Khayal:
The first composition of a khayal performance in slow tempo.

Bandish:
A composition in Hindustani classical music, usually with two parts corresponding to the sthayi and antara.
